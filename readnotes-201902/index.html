¶Paper reading

¶Front Instabilities Can Reverse Desertification[1]

¶related background knowledge

Patterned vegetation: self-organized pattern, without environment effect. e.g. fir waves, tiger brushes…etc.

Self-organized:

  1. strong dynamical non-linearity, often though not necessarily involving positive and negative feedback
  2. balance of exploitation and exploration
  3. multiple interactions

Turing Pattern Pattern formation -> reaction-diffusion system

¶Structure

  1. General issue - Front propagation is a driver of state transition and functional changes in various fields(embryo development, ecosystem function, plant mortality). 3 aspects of front dynamics
    • dynamics of a single front(propagates or pinned)
    • interaction between fronts (merge or repulsed)
    • front instability that involve a reversal in the direction of front propagation
  2. Front instability: longitudinal & transverse(bacterial growth, tumor growth)
    • Model: three-species Lotka-Volterra model or dryland vegetation model
    • Raised the issue: lack of functional aspects of front instabilities, e.g. reversing degradation process
  3. In this letter:
    • Linear & nonlinear transverse instability of desertification fronts
    • Utilization in inducing self-recovery of degraded landscapes.
    • Reversing desertification processes

¶Some important parts

  • The dynamics of pattern forming at the front propagation
  • Seed dispersal for smooth the front, soil-water diffusion to generate larger bulk curve.

¶Microscopic Theory for Peeling Tape

¶Introduction

  • Peeling off adhesive tapes is an archetypal dynamic behavior for dynamical rupture instability. (Stick-slip motion)
  • Kink occurs periodically at ultrasonic frequencies in both slip phase and peeling is regular at macroscopic scales.
  • Existing transverse crack shares the same property like textured surfaces, or the dislocations in the yielding of crystalline materials. This phenomena indicates the elastic bending energy. The origin of the microinstability and its interaction with the macroscopic one remains an open issue.
  • microinstability amplitude is porpotional to the period (main finding)

¶Synopsis: Sorting Blood Cells via Their Stiffness

A proposed modification to a microfluidic cell-sorting device could separate cells by their deformability, an important marker for several diseases. Their simulations show that a squishy red blood cell bends when it bumps into a diamond- or triangular-shaped post, and this bending causes the cell to hug the post and stay on course. In contrast, an unbending stiff cell bounces off any post it hits and is knocked into an adjacent stream.
