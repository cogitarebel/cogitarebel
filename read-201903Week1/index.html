¶Some Physics building blocks

  • Superfluid
    Without viscosity at the specific condition. E.g. He-4 @2.17K. This phenomena can be explained by Bose-Eisenstein statics to explain because He-4 is Boson. No viscosity, no temperature gradient, and unlimited thermal conductivity. An interesting phenomena is Thermomechanical effect, also called Fountain effect. The phase transition to superfluid also called Lambda transition.

  • Quantum vortex

    1. Topological defect exhibited in superfluids and superconductors.
    2. Experimentally observed in Type-2 superconductors, liquid helium, and atomic gases(Bose-Einstein condensate), and optical vortex, exciton-polariton superfluids.

  • Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC)

    Quantum state for all atoms are at the same state, this status is called BEC.

  • Mach-Zehnder interferometer

  • Photoluminescence (PL)

    Matters absorb photons and get excited. Later, those matte at the high energy state back to original (or lower) states with emitted photons. Fluorescence and Phosphorescence are common PL phenomena.
